Privacy Policy

Last updated: 17 July 2026

How Drill Software Limited collects, uses, shares and protects personal data in connection with Assurepage.

01

About this policy

Assurepage is a customer assurance platform that brings information from the tools an organisation already uses into a single customer assurance page.

This Privacy Policy explains how Drill Software Limited ("Drill Software", "we", "us" or "our") collects, uses, shares and protects personal data in connection with Assurepage, our website at https://Assurepage.com, and related support services.

Drill Software Limited is a company registered in England and Wales under company number 16102018.

For questions about this policy or our use of personal data, contact:

  • Data Protection Officer: Nic Coates
  • Email: help@Assurepage.com
02

Our role

Under data protection law, an organisation may act as a "controller", which determines why and how personal data is used, or as a "processor", which processes personal data on another organisation's instructions.

Drill Software acts as a controller when we use personal data to operate our business, administer accounts, provide customer support, communicate with users, protect Assurepage, and meet our legal obligations.

When a business customer uses Assurepage to process personal data under its own direction, that customer is normally the controller and Drill Software acts as its processor. In those circumstances, the customer's privacy notice governs its use of the personal data and our processing is governed by our agreement with that customer. Questions or requests relating to such data should normally be directed to the relevant customer.

03

Who Assurepage is for

Assurepage is a business-to-business service. It is not intended for children or anyone under the age of 18, and we do not knowingly collect personal data from children.

The service is offered to businesses in the United Kingdom, European Union, United States, Australia and New Zealand.

04

Personal data we collect

Depending on how you interact with Assurepage, we may collect:

  • Account and contact information: your email address, which is required to use the service, and your name if you choose to provide it.
  • Communications: information contained in support requests, messages, feedback and other correspondence with us.
  • Service information: information obtained from business customers, integrations, connected services or public sources where a customer configures Assurepage to use those sources. Where we handle this information only on a customer's instructions, we act as its processor.
  • Payment status: information needed to administer a subscription and confirm that payment has been made. Payments may be handled by a payment provider. We do not collect or store full payment-card details.
  • Session information: a strictly necessary session identifier stored in a cookie to keep an authenticated user signed in.

We do not use Assurepage to collect telephone numbers, precise location, advertising identifiers, or special-category data such as health, biometric, racial or ethnic, political, religious, trade-union or sexual-orientation information. We do not intentionally collect criminal-offence data.

We do not use analytics, advertising tracking or session-replay tools, and we do not use personal data to train artificial-intelligence models.

06

Automated decision-making

We do not use personal data to make solely automated decisions that produce legal or similarly significant effects. If this changes, we will update this policy and provide any information and safeguards required by law, including a means of obtaining human review where applicable.

07

Cookies

Assurepage uses a strictly necessary cookie to maintain a user's logged-in session. This cookie expires after seven days or is deleted when the user signs out, whichever happens first.

We do not use optional analytics or advertising cookies. Because the session cookie is necessary to provide a service requested by the user, we do not ask for consent for it. If we introduce non-essential cookies, we will provide appropriate information and choices before using them.

08

How we share personal data

We may share personal data with:

  • service providers that support hosting, infrastructure, authentication, payments, communications and customer support;
  • professional advisers such as accountants, auditors, insurers and legal advisers;
  • regulators, courts, law-enforcement bodies or other authorities where disclosure is required or permitted by law;
  • a prospective buyer, investor or successor if we are involved in a merger, financing, reorganisation or sale of all or part of our business, subject to appropriate confidentiality protections; and
  • a business customer where we process the data in providing Assurepage to that customer.

Our current service providers and subprocessors are listed on the Assurepage Assurance page. We require service providers to protect personal data and use it only for the services they provide to us.

We do not sell personal data or share it with data brokers or third parties for their own behavioural advertising.

09

International transfers

Our servers are located in the European Union. Some suppliers or authorised personnel may access personal data from the United States or other countries outside the United Kingdom.

Where a transfer is restricted by applicable data protection law, we use an approved transfer mechanism as required. Depending on the destination and recipient, this may include applicable adequacy regulations or approved contractual safeguards, such as the UK International Data Transfer Agreement or UK Addendum. We also assess and apply supplementary protections where appropriate.

Contact us at help@Assurepage.com if you would like more information about the safeguards used for a particular transfer.

10

Data retention

We retain account and service information while a subscription remains active. When a subscription is cancelled and the account is closed, we delete the associated account and customer data from active systems.

Limited information may be retained for longer where necessary to:

  • meet legal, tax, accounting or regulatory obligations;
  • establish, exercise or defend legal claims;
  • prevent fraud or misuse; or
  • complete deletion from secure backups as they cycle out under our backup-retention schedule.

Where we retain information for one of these reasons, we restrict it to the relevant purpose and delete or anonymise it when it is no longer needed.

11

Security

We use appropriate technical and organisational measures designed to protect personal data against accidental or unlawful loss, alteration, disclosure, access or destruction. Information about our security programme and controls is available on the Assurepage Assurance page.

No online service can guarantee absolute security. You are responsible for maintaining the security of your own email account, devices and access to Assurepage.

12

Your data protection rights

Depending on where you live and the circumstances, you may have the right to:

  • request access to your personal data;
  • ask us to correct inaccurate or incomplete data;
  • ask us to delete your personal data;
  • restrict or object to certain processing;
  • receive certain data in a portable format;
  • withdraw consent at any time where processing is based on consent; and
  • complain to a data protection regulator.

Some account information can be accessed, corrected, exported or deleted within Assurepage. You may also exercise your rights by emailing help@Assurepage.com. We may request information reasonably necessary to verify your identity, normally by confirming control of the email address associated with the account. We will not use social-media information as the sole method of identity verification.

These rights are not absolute, and an exemption may apply in some circumstances. We will explain our decision if we cannot fulfil a request.

If Drill Software processes the relevant data solely on behalf of one of our business customers, we may refer your request to that customer or assist it in responding.

13

Marketing choices

You can opt out of marketing communications at any time by using the unsubscribe option in the message or contacting help@Assurepage.com. Opting out of marketing will not stop essential service, security, billing or account communications.

14

Complaints

Please contact us first if you have a concern so that we can try to resolve it.

You also have the right to complain to the UK Information Commissioner's Office (ICO). Information about making a complaint is available at https://ico.org.uk/make-a-complaint/.

If you are located outside the United Kingdom, you may also be entitled to complain to the data protection or privacy regulator where you live.

15

Third-party services and links

Assurepage may link to or integrate with third-party websites and services. Those third parties control their own privacy practices, and their privacy notices apply to information they collect for their own purposes. We encourage you to review those notices.

16

Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes to Assurepage, our suppliers or applicable law. We will publish the updated version on our website and revise the date at the top. If a change materially affects how we use personal data, we will provide additional notice where required.
